Autumn


Asian Styling

For this season we have taken inspiration from Asian silhouettes - from the Tibetan chuba, worn on the high Tibetan plateau during the freezing winter months, from the kimono, with its clean, straight lines and wide, swinging sleeves and from Japanese trousers, with their really wide legs and cropped length.

Kimono Colour Block Pullover

Kimono sleeved pullover in a colour blocked, heathery lambswool. Worn with pleated midi skirt in a soft wool blend and hand made, wingcap brogue Chelsea boots in a grained calf leather.

Soft Wool Wrap Jacket

Swingy wrap jacket in a textured wool blend. Layered over a fine wool tencel polo neck and wide-leg, ankle-skimming trousers.

Quilted Miho Jacket

Oversized quilted coat – the first TOAST has made – in a soft wool blend flannel with contrasting gingham lining. Light and cosy, for winter months. Worn with wide-leg, ankle-skimming trousers and a fine wool tencel polo neck.

Cambodian Ikat Kimono Gown

Opulent kimono gown in a soft, lightweight, cotton/silk ikat with wide, square-cut sleeves and a contrasting lining in a sheeny gold viscose. The design based on a traditional Cambodian pattern.
